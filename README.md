# Business Problem
MasterControl provides quality and manufacturing software for life sciences companies through two products that we were asked to focus on: Qx for quality management and Mx for manufacturing operations. While Qx is a mature, high-performing product with over 20 years in the market and a 19.7% conversion rate, Mx—launched four years ago lags behind at 12.7%. The main challenge is not just Mx’s relative newness, but a lack of clarity around its ideal customer profile, including which industries, company types, and job roles are most likely to convert. Buyer profiles successful for Qx do not always translate well to Mx. This misalignment has led to lower conversions, less effective outreach, and missed growth and cross-selling opportunities. The goal of the analysis is to identify high converting accounts and job titles to enable a more targeted, data-driven sales strategy.

# Business Value
Our model created a way for MasterControl to focus on better conversion opportunites in a tiered manner. They will be more productive with their outreach and may be able to limit the number of workers needed to perform these tasks. Generating more revenue and cutting costs allows MasterControl to keep growing and providing helpful solutions to potential clients who actually want it and need it. The model scored every Mx lead and identified 310 Tier 1 accounts — the highest confidence
targets based on account structure, manufacturing context, and buyer role combined. That group converts at 32.9% versus the 13.5% baseline — a nearly 20 point lift and roughly 2.5 times better performance. And to put that in context — 65% of current Mx leads are in Tier 3, converting below baseline at 9.2%. That's where most SDR effort is going today. This is purely about redirecting effort toward what's already in the pipeline. Pilot it, validate the lift, and scale.
